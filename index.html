My Research

My research focuses on computational imaging system with meta-optics, combining metalens imaging system design, wave propagation modeling, computational image reconstruction with deep learning–based algorithms as well as experiment testing.

I began my research during my undergraduate studies at Sun Yat-sen University, where I worked on integrated photonics device design.

A detailed summary of my research experience is available on my LinkedIn profile.

Publications

Adapted from Wang et al., Nature Communications, 2025

A Golay Metalens for Long-Range, Large-Aperture Thermal Imaging via Sparse Aperture Computational Imaging
Jing Wang, Anna Wirth-Singh, Vishwanath Saragadam, Rose Johnson, Arka Majumdar, Ashok Veeraraghavan
Nature Communications, 2025
paper / BibTeX

We present a Golay sparse-aperture metalens computational imaging approach that overcomes the bulk, weight, and cost limitations of conventional long-range mid-wave infrared (MWIR) optics, achieving high-resolution, near-diffraction-limited imaging in a compact and lightweight form factor.
