My Research

My research focuses on computational imaging system with meta-optics, combining metalens imaging system design, wave propagation modeling, computational image reconstruction with deep learning–based algorithms as well as experiment testing. I enjoy tackling complex problems in optical and computational imaging, combining physics, algorithm design, and experimental techniques, and contributing to collaborative research environments.

I began my research during my undergraduate studies at Sun Yat-sen University, where I worked on integrated photonics device design. This experience helped me realize how much I enjoy research, which led me to pursue a Ph.D.

Publications

 A Golay Metalens for Long-Range, Large-Aperture Thermal Imaging via Sparse Aperture Computational Imaging
Jing Wang, Anna Wirth-Singh, Vishwanath Saragadam, Rose Johnson, Arka Majumdar, Ashok Veeraraghavan
Nature Communications, 2025
paper / DOI

We present a Golay-coded metalens combined with sparse aperture computational imaging to enable long-range, large-aperture thermal imaging with significantly reduced system complexity.
